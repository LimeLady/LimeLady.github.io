Data Analytics & Dashboarding

One of the projects I am most proud of is the Google Sheets and Looker Studio integration I did for Kirkman Company. By connecting the API of their CRM and ERP systems (AFAS & Hubspot) to Looker Studio using Apps Scripts, I was able to create a dashboard that gives realtime insights into their project planning and financials as well as employee performance. Whereas I frequently work in Python and R for data manipulation, this project involved complex data connections and transformation within the dashboard using SQL. It also involved thorough communication with multiple stakeholders to understand their needs and KPIs.

Another project I am proud of is the Tableau dashboard I created during my Master's at the UvA for TNO. Together with a team of 4, we analyzed and modeled location data and Tweets from Black Lives Matter demonstrations in the US. We used NLP techniques to analyze the sentiment of the tweets and created a dashboard that visualizes the interaction and sentiment and location of the tweets. This project was complex in the large amount of data and in correctly visualizing the modeled clusters.

Research & Theses

I completed two Master’s theses in Data Science, focusing on distinct but innovative topics. The first, at the Daan Theeuwes Center for Neurorehabilitation, involved modeling recovery trajectories in neurorehabilitation using Dynamic Time Warping to effectively cluster patient subgroups. This research has potential implications for precision medicine, paving the way for its application in larger patient populations. My second thesis, completed during an internship at GroupM, addressed the challenge of social biases in AI-driven image generation. Through a user-centric study, I evaluated the effectiveness of gender bias mitigation in these systems within an advertising context, finding that the utility of these systems not only depends on the diversity and quality of the images produced but also on the varied backgrounds and expectations of users. This work emphasizes the critical role of incorporating diverse user perceptions in the design and evaluation of AI systems, particularly in marketing applications.
